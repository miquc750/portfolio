"Speedety: Crafting Vintage Elegance Through Branding" is a standout project from my MPS in Communication Design at Parsons, showcasing how strategic branding can transform the vintage car rental experience. This initiative doesn't just offer a service; it builds a brand that fuses the charm of classic automobile culture with modern design elegance.

The project begins with an exploration of the unique stories of vintage cars, where design, luxury, and innovation converge. Speedety's branding strategy makes these narratives central, enhancing the user's emotional and visual connection to the era of classic motoring. The brand identity, shaped through extensive research and creative exploration, appeals to both nostalgia and contemporary taste.

Central to Speedety's effort is its visual identity, which includes a carefully chosen logo, color palette, typography, and imagery that evoke vintage luxury and sophistication. The website and user interface design provide a seamless experience, reinforcing Speedety's commitment to merging timeless elegance with modern usability. This project not only revisits the past but also secures Speedety's place as a curator of unique, memorable experiences in the car rental market.
